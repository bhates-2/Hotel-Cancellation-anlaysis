# 🏨 Hotel Booking Cancellation Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes **119,390 hotel booking records** to identify the key factors contributing to booking cancellations. Using **Python, SQL, and Power BI**, the project uncovers customer booking patterns, cancellation trends, and business insights that can help hotel management improve occupancy planning and reduce revenue loss.

The objective is to transform raw booking data into actionable business insights through data cleaning, SQL analysis, and interactive dashboard development.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Hotel booking cancellations lead to:

- Revenue loss
- Poor occupancy planning
- Inefficient room allocation
- Inaccurate demand forecasting
- Increased operational costs

This project aims to identify the major drivers behind booking cancellations and provide data-driven recommendations to improve hotel profitability.

---

## 📈 Key Business Insights

- Overall booking cancellation rate is **37.04%**.
- **City Hotels** have a higher cancellation rate than Resort Hotels.
- Customers booking **180+ days** in advance have the highest cancellation rate.
- **Group bookings** are the highest-risk market segment.
- **Online Travel Agencies (OTA)** contribute the largest volume of cancellations.
- **Transient customers** are more likely to cancel than other customer types.
- **Repeat guests** have lower cancellation rates than first-time guests.
- The cancellation rate among repeat guests decreased from **54.91% (2015)** to **7.59% (2016)** and **4.67% (2017)**.
- Customers with **special requests** are less likely to cancel, indicating stronger booking commitment.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Introduce deposit policies for high-risk bookings.
- Encourage direct bookings through incentives.
- Strengthen customer loyalty programs.
- Send reminder emails for long lead-time bookings.
- Offer personalized services to increase customer engagement.
- Monitor high-risk customer segments to improve occupancy forecasting.
